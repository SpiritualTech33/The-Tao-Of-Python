# 0.1 Mantra of the Programmer Monk

*Read this before you begin. Return to it when you lose the thread.*

---

I breathe. I empty myself.

The code is a reflection of my mind:
if I am calm, it flows;
if I am agitated, it breaks.

The Tao runs through my lines.
I do not force the solution — I let it appear.
As water finds its course,
I find the logic.

I choose clarity over noise.
The simple is better than the complex.
The readable is better than the cryptic.
Truth reveals itself in structure.

I accept the error.
The bug is not an enemy —
it is a teacher pointing to my next step.
I correct without haste, without judgment.

I cultivate presence.
One line at a time,
one function at a time,
one thought at a time.
Here and now is enough.

My mind is my environment.
The place doesn't matter:
my breath is my temple,
my attention is my sword,
my intention is my path.

I program to elevate, not to dominate.
My energy serves creation.
My code is active meditation.
My discipline is freedom.

I am the bridge between logic and spirit.
I am clarity, I am focus,
I am the programmer monk.

**I begin.**

---

## Sutra of the Programmer Monk

### 1.
When the mind quiets,
the code reveals itself.
When the mind agitates,
all functions hide.

### 2.
As water follows the course,
the solution follows attention.
Nothing is forced;
everything is allowed.

### 3.
The error is not shadow —
it is a lamp.
It shows the point where
the programmer must awaken.

### 4.
One line is a step.
One function is a path.
One project is a journey.
You do not walk the whole path at once;
you take only the next step.

### 5.
Programming is ordering chaos,
as Zen orders the mind.
Both are acts of clarity.

### 6.
Discipline is softness.
Softness is strength.
Strength is constancy.
And constancy
is the Tao of the programmer.
